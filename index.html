Lunar Cratering Over Time

Introduction

Our project analyzes how lunar craters survive or are erased over time in different types of terrain. We focus on two major surface types: mare (dark volcanic plains) and non-mare highlands. Using simulated crater catalogs, we distinguish between craters that survived to the end of the model run and those that were erased by later impacts or resurfacing. Our goal is to compare spatial patterns and size distributions of craters in mare versus non-mare regions.
